Dying Alone; The Badness of Death — Death

Shelly Kagan

A warm, surprisingly accessible lecture on one of philosophy's stranger puzzles: can death be "bad" for you, if by the time it happens, there's no you left to be harmed by it?

Film·Modern, 2014·2h 29m

Gone Girl

David Fincher

A wife fakes her own death to punish a husband who stopped playing the part of a devoted spouse. It's dark and cynical, but the question underneath is real: in any long relationship, how much of what you show each other is genuine, and how much is just the role you've agreed to play?

Symphony No. 5

Ludwig van Beethoven

Four notes, repeated relentlessly, like something pounding at the door. Beethoven wrote it while going deaf himself, unable to hear the very piece that would outlive him by centuries.

The Ambassadors

Hans Holbein the Younger

Two powerful, successful men, painted with every symbol of their worldly achievement. At their feet is an odd smear that makes no sense head-on — step to the side, and it resolves into a skull.
