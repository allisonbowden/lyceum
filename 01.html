Truth, Illusion & Deception

How do we know what's real when someone else controls what we're shown — and how do we handle seeing things differently than we expected?

The Ring of Gyges: Morality and Hypocrisy — Philosophy and the Science of Human Nature

Tamar Gendler

Imagine a ring that makes you invisible. Would you still act the same way? Plato uses this to ask whether you're good because you choose to be, or just because someone's watching.

Book·Classic, c. 375 BCEsyllabus·~20 min read

The Republic — Book VII, "The Allegory of the Cave"

Plato

Prisoners chained in a cave mistake shadows on the wall for reality itself. This single chapter, the most famous part of the whole book, asks what happens the moment one of them turns around and sees the fire behind him. You don't need the rest of The Republic to get the idea.
