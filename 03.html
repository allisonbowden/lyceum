Power, Justice & The State

Who gets to make the rules for everyone else, and what do you do when they're wrong?

All five works ≈ 9.5 hr, or ≈ 8 hr if you skip the book

Lecture·Harvard, 2005·55 min

The Moral Side of Murder — Justice

Michael Sandel

A runaway trolley is about to kill five people. You can pull a switch and save them, but it will kill one person instead. What do you do? Sandel opens his course with this question to show how quickly our own moral reasoning contradicts itself.

The Godfather, Parts I & II

Francis Ford Coppola

A son takes over the family business and slowly becomes everything his father swore he'd never be. Watched together, the two films ask a harder question than the first one alone: how does a good man become a bad one, one reasonable decision at a time?

Book·Classic, 1513syllabus·~2 hr read

The Prince

Niccolò Machiavelli

Machiavelli asks a blunt question: should a leader be good, or just look good? His answer: if you can't have both, choose feared over loved. Five hundred years later, ask yourself: does any leader you actually admire disagree?

Music·Classic, 1976syllabus·12 min

Zombie

Fela Kuti & Africa 70

A song that compares Nigerian soldiers to mindless "zombies" blindly following orders, deliberately unsubtle. In response, the military raided Kuti's compound; his mother died from injuries she suffered in that raid. This is what it can cost to challenge power directly.

Art·1787syllabus·The Met, New York

The Death of Socrates

Jacques-Louis David

Socrates, tried and legally condemned by his own city for asking too many questions, reaches for the poison himself rather than renounce what he believes, while the men around him can barely watch. The state can make something legal, and that doesn't make it right.
